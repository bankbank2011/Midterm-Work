Sim Racing: My Favourite Hobby

A Hobby that sounds boring, but fun when you're invested

Why I Realllllly Love Sim Racing...

I've always been a huge fan of cars and racing ever since I was a child, and now when I sim race it gives me that sense of adrenaline, fun, and a realistic experience from the comforts of home. The immersive nature of modern sim racing platforms like Asseto Corsa, Iracing, F1 25 and countless others allows you to compete against drivers worldwide, learn racing lines, and develop skills that translate to real-world driving. Whether it's Formula 1, GT racing, or endurance events, there's always a new challenge to master.

How I got started

I discovered sim racing from YouTube and felt it was incredibly engaging, so I began with basic gear and gradually upgraded as I learned. Starting small is crucial for learning on driving fundamentals first rather than investing in expensive hardware immediately. I spent countless hours perfecting my braking points, understanding tire management, and studying racing lines. The learning curve is steep but incredibly rewarding, especially when you finally nail a perfect lap or secure a podium finish in an online race against other people with the same interests.

The Things I use to Sim Race

  1. My Laptop that meets the requirements for the game, allowing smooth frame rates and realistic physics simulation
  2. An external monitor which is way bigger and way more smoother than my laptop's screen, providing better visibility and immersion during races
  3. Logitech G29 Steering Wheel and Pedal set, or my Fanatec Clubsport Racing Wheel F1 Edition for authentic force feedback and precise control

Essentials needed to sim race

  • Wheel and pedals: the foundation of any sim racing setup
  • Wheelbase or force feedback unit essential for feeling the road and improving racecraft
  • Rig or stable cockpit setup ensures consistent positioning and reduces fatigue during long races
  • PC or console capable of running your chosen sim at high settings and frame rates
  • Optional shifter, handbrake, and motion accessories for enhanced realism and immersion
