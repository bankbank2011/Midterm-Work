Piano

Piano has been with me since childhood and it's my go-to way to relax. I practise mostly on a CN201 — it's the keyboard I use for most sessions. On weekends I’ll sit down for longer practice runs, working on classical pieces, pop songs, and covers of Japanese tracks I learn from YouTube. The piano helps me switch off, and I like how steady progress shows up when I keep a routine.

My practice routine is simple and repeatable: warm up with scales and arpeggios, tackle the hardest bars in short focused bursts, then run through the piece slowly to bring everything together. When I have time I do longer sessions, but even short daily practice helps.

What I use

I use a CN201 digital keyboard. It’s portable, has a nice key action for the price, and a clear tone that suits both classical practice and pop covers.

Goals

  • Improve sight-reading and accuracy for classical pieces
  • Learn a new pop or anime cover every month
  • Record short practice clips to track progress
